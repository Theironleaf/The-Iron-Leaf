Meta-AnalysisNutrition · Protein
★★★★★
PLANT PROTEIN & MUSCLE HYPERTROPHY

Systematic review of 18 randomised controlled trials comparing plant and animal protein sources for resistance-training-induced muscle growth. Findings show statistically equivalent hypertrophy outcomes when leucine thresholds are met and total daily protein intake is matched between groups.

Messina et al. (2021) · Sports Medicine Read in Context
Systematic ReviewNutrition · Gut Health
★★★★
DIETARY FIBRE & ATHLETIC PERFORMANCE

Evidence linking high dietary fibre intake to improved recovery markers, reduced systemic inflammation, and enhanced aerobic performance through gut microbiome diversity. Short-chain fatty acid production from fibre fermentation identified as a key mechanistic pathway.

Spector et al. (2019) · Nature Medicine Read in Context
Cohort StudyLongevity · Cardiorespiratory Fitness
★★★★★
VO2 MAX AS A LONGEVITY PREDICTOR

Large cohort analysis (n=122,007) establishing cardiorespiratory fitness as the strongest modifiable predictor of all-cause mortality — stronger than smoking cessation, hypertension management, or diabetes control. Each MET increase associated with measurable mortality risk reduction.

Mandsager et al. (2018) · JAMA Network Open Read in Context
Randomised Controlled TrialMobility · Joint Health
★★★
DAILY MOBILITY PRACTICE & JOINT LONGEVITY

12-week randomised trial demonstrating significant improvements in hip and ankle range of motion, pain reduction, and movement quality with daily structured mobility practice compared to a no-intervention control group.

Behm & Chaouachi (2011) · European Journal of Applied Physiology Read in Context
Meta-AnalysisStrength · Training Volume
★★★★★
DOSE-RESPONSE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN VOLUME & HYPERTROPHY

Comprehensive meta-analysis establishing the weekly volume landmarks for muscle hypertrophy — identifying 10–20 sets per muscle group weekly as the optimal range for most trained individuals, with diminishing returns beyond this threshold.

Schoenfeld et al. (2017) · Journal of Sports Sciences Read in Context
Randomised Controlled TrialStrength · Training Intensity
★★★★
PROXIMITY TO FAILURE & HYPERTROPHY OUTCOMES

Comparing training to failure versus training 1–3 reps short of failure across matched volume. Results show equivalent hypertrophy with significantly reduced neuromuscular fatigue and faster recovery in the non-failure group.

Refalo et al. (2021) · Journal of Sports Sciences Read in Context
Meta-AnalysisNutrition · Soy & Hormones
★★★★★
SOY ISOFLAVONE INTAKE & MALE TESTOSTERONE

Analysis of 41 studies examining the relationship between soy protein and isoflavone consumption and male reproductive hormones. No significant effect found on testosterone, oestrogen, or sex hormone-binding globulin at normal dietary intake levels.

Reed et al. (2021) · Reproductive Toxicology Read in Context
Systematic ReviewRecovery · Sleep Science
★★★★★
SLEEP DURATION & ATHLETIC RECOVERY MARKERS

Review of sleep extension studies in athletic populations showing measurable improvements in reaction time, sprint performance, and mood with sleep duration increases of 1–2 hours above habitual baseline.

Walker (2017) · Why We Sleep, supporting literature review Read in Context
Cohort StudyLongevity · Sarcopenia
★★★★
RESISTANCE TRAINING & AGE-RELATED MUSCLE LOSS

Longitudinal data demonstrating that consistent resistance training significantly slows sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) compared to sedentary controls, with effects observable even when training begins after age 60.

Peterson et al. (2011) · Ageing Research Reviews Read in Context
Observational StudyNutrition · Microbiome Diversity
★★★★
PLANT FOOD DIVERSITY & MICROBIOME RICHNESS

The American Gut Project found that consuming more than 30 different plant species weekly was the single strongest dietary predictor of gut microbiome diversity — more significant than any specific food category alone.

McDonald et al. (2018) · mSystems Read in Context
